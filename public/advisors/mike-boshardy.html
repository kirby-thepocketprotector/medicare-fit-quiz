About

Meet Mike

Mike Boshardy is a licensed Medicare attorney and Senior Advisor at The Pocket Protector with over a decade of experience across Medicare law, regulatory compliance, and benefits advisory. His legal and operational background gives him a perspective on Medicare that very few advisors can match — he doesn't just understand the plans, he understands the regulations behind them, the compliance frameworks that govern them, and the fine print that most people never read.

Mike began his Medicare career as Associate Counsel at GoHealth, where he was part of the team that helped scale the company from a small operation to a publicly traded national platform. That experience gave him deep, hands-on knowledge of how Medicare distribution works at scale — from carrier relationships and plan structures to the enrollment process itself. He then served as VP of Legal and Chief Compliance Officer at Advantmed, a Medicare-focused healthcare data company, where he oversaw regulatory compliance and gained an even deeper understanding of how coverage decisions are made behind the scenes.

At The Pocket Protector, Mike brings that expertise directly to clients. He specializes in complex Medicare situations where a legal or regulatory understanding makes the difference — guaranteed issue rights, enrollment timing, coordination of benefits, appeals, and coverage disputes. When a client's situation involves more than just picking a plan, Mike is the advisor who can navigate it.

Mike is also uniquely skilled in life settlements, helping seniors unlock the real cash value of life insurance policies they no longer need — rather than letting them lapse or surrendering them back to the insurance company for a fraction of what they're worth. For clients concerned about retirement funding, Mike connects Medicare planning with their broader financial picture to make sure no dollar is left on the table.

Mike holds a Juris Doctor from the University of Wisconsin Law School. Outside of work, he is a supporter of pediatric healthcare research, having funded a data analytics core at Lurie Children's Hospital in Chicago in honor of his late wife.
