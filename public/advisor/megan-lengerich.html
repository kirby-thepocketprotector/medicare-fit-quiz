About

Meet Megan

Megan Lengerich is a Senior Medicare Advisor at The Pocket Protector with over 25 years of experience serving seniors across healthcare, insurance, and long-term care. A United States Air Force veteran, Megan began her career as a Clinical Medical Technician with the 14th Medical Group at Columbus Air Force Base, where she developed the discipline, precision, and commitment to service that define her advisory practice today.

After her military service, Megan spent over a decade as an Executive Director and Administrator of senior living communities, overseeing all aspects of care, compliance, and operations for long-term care facilities. That hands-on experience gave her a rare, ground-level understanding of what seniors actually face — the coverage gaps, the confusing transitions between Medicaid and Medicare, the unexpected costs that erode retirement savings, and the policies that quietly stop serving their owners. She's seen firsthand what happens when seniors are in the wrong plan or paying for insurance they no longer need.

Megan brings that perspective to every client interaction. She specializes not only in Medicare Advantage, Medigap, and Part D plans, but also in life settlements — helping seniors unlock the real value of life insurance policies they may no longer need rather than letting them lapse or surrendering them back to the insurance company for pennies on the dollar. For clients concerned about retirement funding, Megan connects the dots between their insurance portfolio and their financial reality, ensuring they never give money back to an insurance company when a better option exists.

When Megan is not advising seniors or spending time with her family, she loves fishing.

Widow Nearly Surrendered a $250K Policy for Nothing
A recently widowed client was about to let her husband's $250,000 life insurance policy lapse because she could no longer afford the premiums and didn't think she had options. Her insurance company offered a $3,200 cash surrender value. Megan connected her with a life settlement buyer who paid $85,000 for the policy — money that went directly toward stabilizing her retirement.
$4,800/yr Saved
Medicare — Overpaying After Losing Medicaid
Senior Lost Medicaid and Had No Idea What to Do Next
A client lost her Medicaid eligibility after a small inheritance pushed her over the asset threshold. She was suddenly paying full Medicare premiums plus a standalone Part D plan she didn't need. Megan reviewed her situation, moved her into a Medicare Advantage plan with built-in drug coverage and a Part B giveback, and cut her annual out-of-pocket costs by nearly $4,800.
Retirement Secured
Life Settlement + Medicare Optimization
Veteran Sitting on a Policy He Couldn't Use and a Plan He Was Overpaying For
A veteran was paying $380/month for a whole life policy his children didn't need, plus $310/month in Medicare supplement premiums — despite getting most of his care through the VA. Megan settled the life policy for a lump sum, moved him to a $0-premium MAPD plan with a Part B giveback, and freed up over $700/month that went straight into his retirement account.
